# Ab Initio Structure Prediction of CD8 Alpha Chain

**Author:** Şevval Peköz  
**Institution:** Inonu University - Department of Molecular Biology and Genetics  
**Course:** Molecular Modeling  

## Project Overview

In this project, the 3D structure of the CD8 alpha (CD8A) chain, a critical transmembrane glycoprotein and co-receptor for the T-Cell Receptor (TCR) in cytotoxic T lymphocytes, was modeled *ab initio*. CD8 plays a vital role in the cellular immune response by directly binding to the α3 domain of MHC Class I molecules on target cells. This physical interaction stabilizes the TCR-antigen complex and initiates critical intracellular signaling cascades via the Lck tyrosine kinase, making it a key structural target in immunology.

## Methodology

* The structural modeling was performed using AlphaFold.
* The target sequence (derived from the 1BQH complex) generated a multimeric prediction, and the analysis was specifically focused on isolating the CD8 alpha chain (**Chain D**).
* Among the 5 different conformations generated by the algorithm, `model_0` was selected as the reference structure due to its highest local confidence ranking for the target chain (pLDDT: **81.04**).
* To assess structural accuracy, a sequence-independent alignment was performed in PyMOL. Following a rigorous 5-cycle outlier rejection protocol, the structural superimposition yielded an exceptional **RMSD of 0.700 Å** (across 2693 aligned atoms), confirming high conformational stability and reliability of the selected model.

## Structural Analysis and Visualization

The predicted `.cif` model was imported into **PyMOL** and colored according to the per-residue confidence score (pLDDT) metrics to evaluate regional stability:
* **Blue (pLDDT > 90):** High accuracy expected (Structured core domains, characteristic of the beta-sandwich fold).
* **Cyan/Yellow (90 > pLDDT > 50):** Moderate confidence (Flexible loop regions).
* **Orange (pLDDT < 50):** Intrinsically disordered or highly flexible tail regions.
* **Highlighted Structural Features:** In the high-resolution PyMOL render (`1BQH_pymol_render.png`), the CD8 alpha chain is specifically isolated from the multimeric complex. The visualization highlights its characteristic immunoglobulin-like (Ig-like) extracellular V-type domain, which is structurally responsible for the crucial binding interaction with the MHC Class I complex.
